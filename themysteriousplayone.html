Jess nodded while biting her lip and holding back tears.

Isaiah had been here many times before, back when Jac was still alive. East Tower was the most social of the residential halls. Everyone’s suite door was open, except for room 809. Jac’s room. Isaiah knocked. No answer. He twisted the knob and ventured inside.

Upon entering, they were greeted by a large pile of sneakers and Jordans, carelessly kicked off their owner’s feet. Hoodies and Obey snapbacks were strewn across the couch, directly across a flimsy table that seemed to bend with the weight of the bongs and vapes on top of it. There was a solitary, clear spot on the table where Jac would rest his feet during Call of Duty sessions.

Isaiah: Hello?
No answer. Roommates weren’t home.
Jess: Jac?
Isaiah rolled his eyes.

Then the two of them opened the door to Jac’s porch and leaned on the railing, staring out into the starless, night sky. They leaned because there were three chairs to sit in and only two people. And although they were silent, they both secretly hoped Jac would show up. So they kept waiting with their cigarettes in hand, and made clouds… the only type of clouds that can be seen at night.
